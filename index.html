Introduction to my portfolio and background.

Field of Study

Currently I am studying Computer Engineering in the Swanson School of Engineering at Pitt. I am also minoring in Korean. I am in the final semester of my senior year, mainly finishing up general education requirements and technical electives. Throughout my studies at Pitt, I have not changed my major; however, I have refined the field I would like to pursue after graduation. Originally, I was interested in embedded software development, but over time I have developed a stronger interest in hardware engineering, particularly PCB design.

Hobbies & Interests

I enjoy tinkering with electronics and building small projects, often involving microcontrollers and hands-on design. Another major interest of mine is music—I am self-taught on both guitar and piano, and recently I have been focusing more on improving my piano skills. I also enjoy ice hockey, especially following the Pittsburgh Penguins. In the past, I played on a recreational beer league team, which was a great experience that I still miss.

Diversity Statement

I value diversity as an essential component of both personal growth and effective collaboration. Working with individuals from different backgrounds, perspectives, and experiences leads to more creative problem-solving and stronger outcomes. Through my academic and personal experiences, I strive to foster an inclusive environment where all voices are respected and considered. I am committed to continuous learning, open-mindedness, and contributing to communities that support equity and inclusion.
